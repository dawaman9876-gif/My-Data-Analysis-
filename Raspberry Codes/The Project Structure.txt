                                                             # Power Wiring Instructions #

Raspberry Pi Power:
1. Use a dedicated 5V/3A Micro-USB power supply. Do not power the Pi from the motor driver.
2. Motor Driver Power (CRUCIAL):
             = Connect a 12V Battery Pack (e.g., 8x AA batteries or a LiPo pack) to the VIN and GND terminals on the L298N.
             = Remove the 5V Enable Jumper from the L298N module. This prevents it from trying to power the Pi.
             = Connect the GND terminal of the L298N to the Raspberry Pi's GND (any pin). This establishes a common ground.

3. Sensor & Relay Power:
            = The 5V Out pin on the L298N can be used to create a 5V Power Rail for the DHT22, Ultrasonic Sensor, and Relay module. 
              Check that the L298N's 5V regulator can handle the current draw of your sensors and relay. If not, use a separate 5V UBEC.
            = Connect all components' GND pins to the common Ground Rail.

4. Spray Pump Power:
            = The Peristaltic Pump is powered directly by the 12V Battery Pack through the relay. The Raspberry Pi only controls the low-power relay switch, 
               keeping high-current circuits safe and separate.

5. Safety and Best Practices
            = Use a Logic Level Converter (Optional but Recommended): The L298N may not perfectly respect the Pi's 3.3V logic levels. 
              For long-term reliability, use a bi-directional logic level converter between the Pi's GPIO pins and the L298N's control pins (ENA, IN1, IN2, etc.).
            = Fuses: Place a fuse (e.g., 2A) on the positive wire from the 12V battery to the L298N.
            = Wire Gauge: Use thicker wires (e.g., 22 AWG) for motor and battery connections. Thinner wires (e.g., 26 AWG) are fine for sensors.
            = Secure Connections: Use a solderless breadboard for prototyping, but solder all connections for the final robust platform. Consider using a GPIO ribbon 
              cable breakout board for cleaner wiring.
